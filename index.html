I am a PhD Candidate in the Department of Political Science at Stanford University and the Steven and Debi Wisch Fellow at the Center for South Asia for 2026-27. My research examines how religion, group identities, and social hierarchies shape democratic politics, with a regional focus on South Asia, especially India.

My work has been accepted for publication in the Journal of Politics and has been received the Kenneth D Wald Best Graduate Student Paper award from APSA's Religion & Politics section. My research is supported by the National Science Foundation, the King Center on Global Development, the Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies, and the Stanford Institute for Economic Policy Research.

Prior to doctoral studies, I lived and worked in India, Senegal, and Nigeria on development policy projects at IDinsight. I also contributed to Giving Green and conducted screenwriting research for a television show on Indian politics. I hold an MA in Political Science from Stanford and an AB in Economics from Brown University. I speak Hindi, Urdu, Polish and Spanish. I am an avid musician and play both classical guitar and sitar.
